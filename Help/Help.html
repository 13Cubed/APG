APG generates passwords with two (2) to eight (8) random common dictionary words. It displays length and approximate bits of entropy.

FAQ

How many words are included in the default word list?

4,701

What is the origination of the word list?

This word list was obtained from Trinity College Department of Computer Science Cryptology Resources Index. Words less than 4 characters in length and contractions have been removed from the list included in APG.

Can I change the word list used by APG?

Absolutely. Right-click on the APG app icon and choose Show Package Contents. Browse to Contents > Resources and replace the Words.txt file with your own. Most UNIX distributions, including OS X, have a built-in word list of approximately 250,000 words. That word list can be found here: /usr/share/dict/words.

If I replace the word list will the approximate bits of entropy listed still be correct?

APG will evaluate the total number of words in the word list (x) and then evaluate log2(x). The calculation is simply the number of words (n) * log2(x), taking into account the use of any separators.

What random number mechanism does APG use and how is it seeded?

APG uses the newer arc4random() function as opposed to rand() or random(). This function is self-seeding and uses a superior algorithm.
